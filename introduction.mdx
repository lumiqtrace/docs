LumiqTrace is an LLM observability platform built for developers shipping AI applications. Every time your code calls an LLM — whether it's a single chat completion or a complex multi-step agent workflow — LumiqTrace captures the full picture: latency, token usage, cost, errors, and more. The tagline says it simply: illuminate every LLM call.

## What you can do with LumiqTrace

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Distributed tracing" icon="git-branch">
    Follow a user request across every LLM call it touches. The flame graph view shows each span's latency, cost, and status so you can pinpoint exactly where time and money are spent.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Cost tracking" icon="dollar-sign">
    See your LLM spend broken down by model, user, environment, and time period. Cached token discounts are computed automatically using per-model pricing.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Performance monitoring" icon="gauge">
    Track P50, P90, and P99 latency alongside time-to-first-token (TTFT) for streaming calls. Spot slow models and regressions before your users do.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Anomaly detection" icon="triangle-alert">
    A background job scans your metrics every 15 minutes and surfaces statistically significant deviations — cost spikes, latency jumps, error surges — with plain-English explanations.
  </Card>
  <Card title="AI-powered insights" icon="sparkles">
    The Cost Optimizer analyses your 30-day spend and returns ranked, actionable recommendations with estimated monthly savings. Ask your data questions in natural language with NL Query.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Root cause analysis" icon="search">
    For any failed or slow trace, request an AI-generated root cause report that explains what went wrong and how to prevent it.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Who LumiqTrace is for

LumiqTrace is designed for developers and engineering teams who are building products on top of LLMs. If you're calling OpenAI, Anthropic, or Google from your backend — or orchestrating multi-step agents — you need visibility into what's happening at runtime. LumiqTrace gives you that without requiring you to instrument every call by hand. A two-line SDK setup is all it takes.
