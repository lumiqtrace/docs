The AI Hub page brings together four AI-powered features that help you understand and improve your LLM operations without manually sifting through raw data. Three of the features live in the AI Hub's tab interface — Cost Optimizer, Anomalies, and Ask. The fourth, Root Cause Analysis, is triggered directly from the trace flame graph.

<Warning>
  The AI Hub requires the Team plan or higher to access Anomaly Detection and Natural Language Query. Cost Optimizer and Root Cause Analysis require the Pro plan or higher. See the plan details for each feature below.
</Warning>

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Cost Optimizer">

The Cost Optimizer analyzes your last 30 days of LLM spend and produces a ranked list of specific, actionable recommendations for reducing your monthly bill. Each recommendation is generated by an AI model that examines your actual usage patterns — which models you use, how often, at what token volumes, and at what cache hit rates — so the suggestions are tailored to your project, not generic advice.

**Plan availability:** Pro (weekly refresh), Team (daily refresh), Scale (real-time)

### What you see

Each recommendation card shows:

- **Title** — a concise description of the optimization opportunity
- **Description** — an explanation of why this change would reduce cost
- **Category** — the type of optimization (e.g., model switching, caching, batching)
- **Estimated monthly savings** — a dollar figure for how much you could save per month
- **Confidence** — a percentage bar showing how confident the analysis is in this recommendation, based on the strength of the signal in your data
- **Effort** — `low`, `medium`, or `high` — how much implementation work the change requires
- **Tradeoff** — any quality or performance tradeoff you should be aware of
- **Code example** — an expandable snippet showing how to implement the change in your codebase

Recommendations are sorted by estimated monthly savings, so the highest-value changes appear first. Cards with confidence below 60% are filtered out automatically.

### Giving feedback

Each card has a thumbs up / thumbs down button. Use these to tell LumiqTrace whether the recommendation was useful. Your feedback improves future recommendations for your project.

<Tip>
  If a recommendation shows high confidence and low effort, implement it first — it's the easiest win. High-effort recommendations are worth reading even if you don't act on them immediately, since they often reveal architectural patterns in your LLM usage.
</Tip>

  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Anomalies">

The Anomaly Detection tab shows a live feed of statistical anomalies detected in your LLM usage. LumiqTrace scans your metrics every 15 minutes, compares them to your 14-day baseline using Z-score analysis, and flags anything that deviates by more than 2.5 standard deviations. When an anomaly is found, an AI model writes a plain-English explanation of what happened and what it means for your application.

**Plan availability:** Team and Scale only

<Warning>
  Anomaly detection is not available on the Free or Pro plans. If you're on Pro and see the Anomalies tab, the feed will be empty and a prompt to upgrade will appear.
</Warning>

### What anomalies look like

Each anomaly card shows:

- **Severity** — `low`, `medium`, or `high` based on how far the metric deviated from baseline
- **Affected metric** — which measurement triggered the alert: cost, latency, or error rate
- **Affected model** — the specific model where the anomaly was detected (if applicable)
- **Headline** — a one-sentence summary of what changed
- **Explanation** — two to three sentences describing the anomaly in plain English, including how far the value deviated from normal
- **Business impact** — what this anomaly likely means for your users or your bill
- **Recommended action** — a concrete next step to investigate or resolve the issue

### Auto-refresh

The anomaly feed refreshes automatically every 60 seconds. You do not need to reload the page. A timestamp in the top-right corner shows when the data was last updated.

<Note>
  A new anomaly can appear at most every 15 minutes, since that's how often the detection job runs. If the feed is empty, no anomalies were found in recent data — which is the expected state most of the time.
</Note>

  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Ask">

The Ask tab lets you query your LLM usage data by typing a question in plain English. You don't need to know SQL or understand the underlying data schema. Type your question, submit it, and LumiqTrace converts it to a database query, runs it, and returns the results along with an AI-written summary.

**Plan availability:** Team (50 queries per month), Scale (unlimited)

<Warning>
  Natural language queries are not available on the Free or Pro plans. Team plan customers have a limit of 50 queries per calendar month. Scale plan customers have no limit.
</Warning>

### How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Type your question">
    Enter any question about your LLM usage in the text area. Be as specific or as general as you like.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit">
    Click **Ask** or press Enter. LumiqTrace converts your question to a database query and runs it against your project's data.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the results">
    The response appears below the input in three sections: the generated SQL (expandable), a results table with dynamic columns based on your query, and an AI-written summary of what the results mean.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Example questions

- "Which model had the highest error rate last week?"
- "Show me my top 10 most expensive traces"
- "What was my total cost for GPT-4o in March?"
- "How many requests timed out in the last 24 hours, broken down by model?"
- "What's my average latency per model this month?"

<Tip>
  Questions that reference a specific time period ("last week", "in March", "yesterday") produce the most useful results. If you ask a broad question with many results, the AI summary will highlight the most significant findings for you.
</Tip>

## Root cause analysis

Root cause analysis is triggered from inside a trace, not from the AI Hub. When you open a trace in the [Traces page](/dashboard/traces) and click **Explain with AI** in the span detail panel, LumiqTrace analyzes every span in that trace and returns a structured diagnosis.

**Plan availability:** Pro (10 analyses per month), Team (50 per month), Scale (unlimited)

<Warning>
  The **Explain with AI** button is visible on all plans but only functional on Pro and above. Clicking it on the Free plan will prompt you to upgrade.
</Warning>

### What root cause analysis returns

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Summary | A plain-English description of what the trace shows overall |
| Root cause type | A category for the problem, such as `token_limit_exceeded`, `upstream_timeout`, or `rate_limited` |
| Problem span | The specific LLM call identified as the source of the issue |
| Contributing factors | Other spans or conditions in the trace that made the problem worse |
| Fix steps | A prioritized list of actions, each with an implementation note |
| Prevention tip | One recommendation for avoiding this class of problem in the future |

### Timing and caching

Analysis runs asynchronously and takes between 5 and 30 seconds depending on trace complexity. A loading indicator appears in the panel while the analysis runs. Once complete, the results are cached for 48 hours — reopening the same trace within that window shows the cached result instantly, with no additional wait.

<Note>
  Root cause analysis uses an AI model with extended reasoning capability, which provides more diagnostic depth than standard AI responses. The extra latency reflects the model working through the problem before producing its answer.
</Note>
